Phase 1

Set up your store

1. Getting started

Create your account and get the whole team into the dashboard.

  1. 1Go to Sign up free, create your account, and tick the box to accept the Terms & Privacy Policy. No credit card for the 30-day trial.
  2. 2The first account at a rooftop becomes the Owner / Dealer Admin. From Profile & Settings you can invite salespeople and managers by email.
  3. 3Each teammate signs in at the login page and lands on their own dashboard. The left menu is your navigation; the top bar stays fixed as you scroll.
Roles matter: Owner, Dealer Admin and Manager see team-wide tools (leaderboard, appraisals, reports, settings). Salespeople see their own work.

2. Install the Chrome extension

The extension is what fills out Facebook Marketplace for you. Install it once per computer.

  1. 1In the dashboard top bar, click Install extension to open the Chrome Web Store listing, then click Add to Chrome.
  2. 2Pin the MarketSync icon to your toolbar so it's always one click away.
  3. 3Click the icon and log in with the same email/password. The popup now shows your live vehicle queue with a Post button and a safe-to-post indicator on each car.
MarketSync is not a Facebook API. It works through the real Marketplace page in your own browser, so you always stay in control and click Post yourself.

Phase 2

Build your inventory

3. Sync your inventory automatically

  1. 1In Settings → Inventory, add your dealership website or inventory feed URL. You can also import a CSV, or add/edit a car by hand on the Inventory page.
  2. 2MarketSync pulls in every vehicle — photos, specs, price and description — and keeps it current.
  3. 3When a car sells or the price changes on your site, the queue updates so you never post a stale unit. Export the whole book to CSV anytime.

4. Decode a VIN, window stickers & brochures

Inventory Intelligence

  1. 1Open a vehicle and click Decode VIN for a rich breakdown — trim, engine, drivetrain, factory options and more (50+ fields).
  2. 2Generate the factory window sticker to see original MSRP and equipment.
  3. 3Produce a branded dealer brochure AI Boost to hand to shoppers or print for the lot.

5. Scan a licence plate → VIN New

  1. 1On your phone, open the appraisal or VIN tools and use Scan — point the camera at a licence plate or VIN barcode.
  2. 2A plate lookup returns the VIN, then MarketSync decodes the full build automatically — no typing 17 characters in the lane.
  3. 3Works on modern mobile browsers (Chrome and Safari/WebKit), with a barcode-scanner fallback if the camera API isn't available.

6. Pull a Carfax on every car

  1. 1On the Inventory page, every car card has a branded Carfax button beside the brochure button.
  2. 2Click it to open that vehicle's Carfax report directly — no hunting, no extra tabs.
  3. 3Share it with a shopper on the spot to build trust and speed up the deal.

7. Generate AI listing copy — in any language

AI Boost

  1. 1On any vehicle, generate the AI description with one click — MarketSync writes a persuasive, Marketplace-ready listing from the car's specs. Pick SEO, short, long or "freshen" styles.
  2. 2Prefer another language? Use the language selector in the extension to generate copy in French, Spanish or any of 14+ languages.
  3. 3Your chosen language is remembered per rep, so every future listing comes out in it automatically.

8. Photo overlays & AI Vision scoring

  1. 1Turn on photo overlays in settings to stamp your phone number or dealer logo onto listing photos automatically.
  2. 2With AI Vision AI Boost, MarketSync scores every photo for quality and coverage — exterior, interior, dash and engine all shown?
  3. 3It picks the strongest hero image so your best shot leads the listing.

Phase 3

Post & get found

9. Post a vehicle to Facebook Marketplace

  1. 1In the dashboard, click Post to Facebook in the top bar (or open the extension popup). Pick the vehicle you want to list.
  2. 2On Facebook, go to Marketplace → Create new listing → Vehicle, then click Post in the popup.
  3. 3The extension fills every field — year, make, model, mileage, price, description and photos — from your inventory.
  4. 4Review, then click Facebook's Publish. Done in seconds instead of minutes.
Posting-safety guardrails: MarketSync spaces out posts and enforces a sensible daily cap so your Facebook account stays healthy. Safe-to-post cars are marked green.

10. Syndication feed — AutoTrader, Kijiji, Google

  1. 1Open Settings → Integrations and find the Inventory feed card — one live CSV / XML / Google link that always holds your in-stock vehicles.
  2. 2Paste the link into AutoTrader, Trader.ca, Kijiji Autos, CarGurus or Google Merchant Center once — step-by-step guides are built in for each.
  3. 3From then on they pull straight from MarketSync — every new car, price change and sold unit updates automatically. No re-listing.

11. Build your dealer website

  1. 1Open Website in the menu. Use the drag-and-drop builder to arrange sections, set per-page brand colours, and pick your public address.
  2. 2Your synced inventory, Google reviews and an AI sales chat can all appear on the site. Hit Publish and it's live.
  3. 3Website enquiries and chatbot conversations become CRM leads automatically (see the auto-responder, below).

Phase 4

Work leads & customers

12. CRM: contacts, leads, tasks & calendar

The CRM menu (green) opens the page; the arrow beside it expands its sub-items. Every lead, appraisal, sale and service visit lands on one customer record.

  1. 1Search Customers spans the whole store; My Customer Database is the rep's own book. A tag shows whether each contact is a sales and/or service customer.
  2. 2Leads show the assigned salesperson and a hot-lead score so the list sorts by who to call first.
  3. 3Appointments is a month calendar; book one from a customer's record and it shows here. Tasks is your follow-up queue — automation drops tasks here so nothing slips.
  4. 4On the activity timeline, tap ↩ Reply on an inbound message to answer by email (sends) or log a text/call reply.

13. Send leads to your CRM (ADF/XML)

  1. 1In Settings → CRM, add your CRM's lead email or endpoint.
  2. 2New Marketplace and website leads are delivered as standard ADF/XML, so they drop straight into your existing workflow.
  3. 3Keep working leads in MarketSync, your CRM, or both — they stay in sync.

14. Two-way calendar sync New

  1. 1In Settings → Integrations → Calendar sync, click Connect for Google Calendar or Outlook.
  2. 2Every appointment you book in MarketSync appears on the rep's calendar instantly; events they create on that calendar flow back in as appointments.
  3. 3Inbound events also sync every 30 minutes in the background, or hit Sync now anytime.

15. Instant AI lead auto-responder New

AI Boost

  1. 1In Settings → AI, set the auto-responder to Off, Draft (writes a reply for the rep to send) or Auto (sends instantly), by email or SMS.
  2. 2When a website lead comes in, MarketSync answers in seconds with a friendly, on-brand reply — consent and budget rules are always enforced.
  3. 3Speed-to-lead is tracked in the Leads report so you can see how fast leads get answered.

16. Natural-language bulk outreach New

AI Boost

  1. 1Tell the assistant something like "text everyone who looked at a truck in the last 30 days about our weekend sale."
  2. 2MarketSync turns that into a filtered list and a draft message, then shows you a preview to approve before anything sends.
  3. 3Consent and opt-outs are enforced automatically, and sends are capped so you stay compliant.

Phase 5

Close the deal

17. Appraise a trade-in (with disclosure & summary PDFs)

The Trade Appraisal page turns a walk-around into a signed, branded paper trail. Use the Appraise a vehicle button beside Desk a deal, or Appraisals in the menu.

  1. 1Enter or scan the VIN — MarketSync decodes it to pre-fill year, make, model and full build.
  2. 2Fill the Customer Information form and the disclosure — every condition question is on there.
  3. 3Click Notify Appraiser to ping a manager for a number. The internal value range stays with the rep — nothing customer-facing shows the number.
  4. 4Save as PDF: a branded disclosure with signature areas, plus a Customer Summary Sheet. OEM brochure and window-sticker buttons sit right at the top.
Control appraisal visibility per rep in Settings. Won/acquired cars can flow to your website inventory on possession.

18. Desk a deal — estimate, bill of sale & tax

  1. 1Click Desk a deal in the top bar, or open it from any sold/delivered customer record. Search the customer and structure the deal.
  2. 2Tax is auto-filled from the jurisdiction — Canadian HST/GST/PST or a US state base rate, with room to add local/county rates.
  3. 3Print a clean estimate or a full bill of sale, add F&I products, fees and commissions, and set delivery details.
  4. 4Saved values flow straight into the sold-deals report and its CSV export.

19. Vehicle cost & real gross New

  1. 1In Settings → AI/Dealer Management, switch on cost tracking and choose whether sales reps can see cost, or only managers.
  2. 2A cost field appears on the desk (internal only). Front gross then shows on the deal, the Executive ROI dashboard and the Sales report.
  3. 3Cost is never shown to the customer — not on the estimate, bill of sale or any email.

20. Send documents for e-signature New

  1. 1On the deal desk, click Send for e-signature. It sends the exact bill of sale / estimate you generated — no DocuSign needed.
  2. 2The customer opens a secure link, reviews the document, draws and types their signature, and agrees to an e-sign consent — on phone or desktop.
  3. 3You get the signed copy with a signature certificate (time, IP, device). Track sent / viewed / signed from View signature status on the desk.

Phase 6

Service & retention

21. Service department New

  1. 1Open Service in the menu for its settings, an appointments area, and equity mining.
  2. 2Book service appointments against the same customer records as sales — a contact can be tagged as a sales and/or service customer.
  3. 3Customers can book online, and bookings appear on the calendar (and sync to Google/Outlook if connected).

22. Equity mining

  1. 1Open Equity Mining to surface past customers who may be in a positive equity position and ready for an upgrade.
  2. 2Reach out from the customer record, or fold them into a bulk outreach campaign.

23. Automation & follow-up tasks

The Automation page is every automated text & email in one place — flip them on, edit the wording, or rewrite with AI. Compliance kill-switches run behind the scenes.

  1. 1Toggle campaigns across the pipeline, post-delivery retention, reviews, referrals, equity and birthdays/holidays.
  2. 2Sales-rep tasks — turn on Next-Day, 30-Day and 90-Day follow-ups. These drop a task on the lead's assigned rep (in CRM → Tasks) instead of messaging the customer. Off by default.
  3. 3Set global bits (Google review link, referral bonus, service URL, house numbers, business hours) once and every template uses them.

Phase 7

Price, market & grow

24. Inventory Intelligence & Turn Rate

Inventory Intelligence

  1. 1Open Inventory Intelligence for health scores across your lot — what's fresh, what's aging, what needs a price move.
  2. 2Each unit shows a Turn Rate based on days-on-lot, so you can act on slow movers before they cost you.
  3. 3Run the Inventory Scan to refresh every car's "% to market" and flag mispriced units.

25. Price prediction & market snapshot (MarketCheck)

Inventory Intelligence

  1. 1On a vehicle, open the Market Price Report / Lot Average for a live US & Canada price-to-market read, powered by MarketCheck.
  2. 2Open the Market Snapshot to see supply, average price and days-to-sell for that year/make/model in your area, with comparable listings.
  3. 3Use AI price prediction during appraisal to land on a fair number fast. Live lookups have a fair-use daily cap to keep costs predictable.

26. Marketing ROI & ad-spend auto-import New

  1. 1Open the Marketing ROI report to see leads, sales, cost-per-lead, cost-per-sale, revenue and ROI by channel — no tagging needed.
  2. 2Enter spend by hand, or in Auto-import spend connect Meta Ads and Google Ads so monthly spend fills in automatically.
  3. 3Spend lines up with the same channels your leads come from, so ROI is right out of the box.

Phase 8

Measure everything

27. Reports hub & custom builder

The Reports page is manager-only, with a full tab hub plus a quick-access rail on the right of the dashboard.

  1. 1Tabs cover Sales, F&I, Leads & sources, Rep scorecard, Appraisals, Appointments & show-rate, Service, E-signatures, Marketing ROI, Activity and Customers — each range-filterable with CSV export.
  2. 2The Overview tab has the Executive ROI dashboard (with real gross when cost tracking is on), inventory mix & aging, and a custom report builder.
  3. 3The right-side rail (Lot Average, Inventory Scan, Market Snapshot, Lot Health, Speed to Lead, and the rest) jumps straight to any report.

28. Dealership insights on your dashboard New

  1. 1Managers see a Dealership insights block right on the Dashboard: units & gross, appointment show-rate and e-sign completion tiles.
  2. 2Below that: a Marketing-ROI snapshot and the full CRM/lead insights panel — lead volume & trend, sources, pipeline funnel, conversion and a rep leaderboard.

29. Sales leaderboard

  1. 1The leaderboard now lives right on the Dashboard — a podium plus a full ranked table. Your rank chip in the top bar jumps to it.
  2. 2Points come from real desked deals, appraisals and listings. Switch My Team / Global, and run week/month competitions.

30. Ask MarketSync — the AI brain

AI Boost Inventory Intelligence

  1. 1Click the Ask MarketSync bubble. Ask real questions — "Which units are aging 60+ days?", "Anything priced off market?", "How's the RAV4 market?" — and it uses live tools to answer.
  2. 2It can do things too (with a confirm step) — draft a task or a bulk outreach — and it's grounded in your dealer background and a knowledge base you upload in Settings.
  3. 3Set the AI's style (internal vs customer-facing) in Settings, and turn on a proactive weekly briefing. Budget safeguards keep spend in check and keep answers on-topic.

Phase 9

Run the business

31. Teams, roles & dealer groups

Open Settings from the gear icon. Everything lives under tabs: Team, Account, Branding, Language, Billing, AI, Dealer Group, Dealer Management, Integrations, Security.

  1. 1Team — Sales and Management are login users; Service, Admin, Cleanup and Lot are simple name rosters you add and remove.
  2. 2Running multiple rooftops? An Owner/Dealer Admin can manage several stores under one dealer group, with roles controlling what each person sees.

32. Security & activity log New

  1. 1In Settings → Security, turn on 2-step login (2FA) with an authenticator app and save your backup codes.
  2. 2Add a passkey (Face ID / Touch ID / Windows Hello) for passwordless sign-in, and review where you're signed in.
  3. 3Admins get a Dealership activity log — who changed cost visibility, sent a document to sign, exported the customer book or changed a teammate's role, with time and IP.

33. Change your dashboard language

  1. 1Go to Profile & Settings → Language and pick your language — the whole dashboard switches instantly. It's per rep.
  2. 2Your choice is saved and also drives the language of your AI-written Facebook listings.

34. Turn features on or off

  1. 1Go to Settings → Dealer Management → Features.
  2. 2Flip any feature (Website, Automation, Equity Mining, Inventory Intelligence, Appraisals, Service, Reports) off to hide it for your whole store — turn it back on anytime.
  3. 3The left menu is colour-coded by area and starts collapsed — click a section name to open its page, or the arrow to expand its items.
